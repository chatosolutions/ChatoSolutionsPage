Survey calculation methods: part 1

R Drilling

The tangential method in R

Rigoberto Chandomi Vazquez https://www.linkedin.com/in/rigoberto-chandomi-v%C3%A1zquez-79038495/
05-01-2022

There are several methods of computing directional surveys, in this case two methods are rewired: Tangential angle and Average angle

The tangential method uses the inclination and azimuth at the lower survey station assuming to be a straight line. The angles measured at the upper station are not used in the computing. This method is the most inaccurate when the trajectory is changing significantly between stations.

Tangential calcularions:

\[ \Delta North = \Delta MD * sin(I2) * cos(Az2) \]

\[ \Delta East = \Delta MD * sin(I2) * sin(Az2) \]

\[ \Delta TVD = \Delta MD * cos(I2)\]

where $ MD = measured depth between surveys (m)$

$ I2 = Inclination (angle) of lower survey ()$

$ Az2 = azimuth direction of lower survey ()$

R code

First, loading the survey data from CSV file, in this case we have: MD in meters, Inclination and Azimuth in angles, using read.csv tha data is loaded in dataframe format.

survey <- read.csv("Survey.csv")

For this method, two vectors are created, I2 and A2, to Inclination and azimuth of lower survey. With these vectors, just use the equation above to compute $ North$, $ East$ and $ TVD$.

I2 <- survey$INC[2:(nrow(survey))]
A2 <- survey$AZ[2:(nrow(survey))]

survey$TVD <- -c(survey$MD[1], cumsum(diff(survey$MD)*cos(((I2))*pi / 180)))
survey$DN <- c(0, cumsum(diff(survey$MD)*sin(((I2))*pi / 180)*cos(((A2))*pi / 180)))
survey$DE <- c(0, cumsum(diff(survey$MD)*sin((( I2 ))*pi / 180)*sin((( A2 ))*pi / 180)))

Using Plotly package, the survey can be ploted in 3D

library(plotly)

plot_ly(survey, x = ~DE, y = ~DN, z = ~TVD, type = "scatter3d" , mode = "lines")

We can define a Kelly bushing and head values, as a servey start point, and computing the geographic coordinates and TVDSS

X <- 550000
Y <- 2500000
KB <- 33

survey$TVDSS <- survey$TVD + KB
survey$X <- X + survey$DN
survey$Y <- Y + survey$DE
